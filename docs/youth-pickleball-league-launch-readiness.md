# Youth Pickleball League — Launch-Readiness Register

> **This is the operational/financial/legal companion to
> `youth-pickleball-league-blueprint.md`.** The blueprint designed the *product*
> — values, age banding, season format, leveling, badges, conduct & safety. This
> file tracks what's needed to actually **launch a paid season**: the cost model,
> pricing, minimum viable roster, and a prioritized gap register from existential
> (P0) to go-to-market polish (P4). Internal doc — real dollar figures live here
> (the public "price teased, not quoted" rule is a *site-copy* rule, not a
> back-office one).

---

## 1. Purpose

The blueprint answers *what the league is*. This register answers *what it takes
to legally and profitably charge a parent for it*. It captures the cost,
pricing, and minimum-roster decisions we settled this session, then lays out a
single prioritized gap list so the path from "designed" to "first paid cohort"
is unambiguous: clear the P0 tier before taking a dollar, validate demand cheaply
in parallel, and only flip the switch on a branded paid "league season" once the
existential items are in hand.

## 2. Cost model (settled this session)

- **Court cost is real**, pulled from NGA's own CUPF / ActiveMONTGOMERY pricing:
  **$17/hr per court at the "Court For-Profit C" tier** (NGA is classified
  for-profit by ActiveMONTGOMERY). A 2-hour session therefore = **$34/court**.
- **Staffing:** Sam coaches (a paid wage is carried in the model); **two parent
  volunteers per season** handle check-in, scoring, and reffing — this replaces a
  paid assistant. *Caveat:* volunteers need the same background-check / SafeSport
  vetting as mentors (~$25–50 one-time each, reusable across seasons) plus a short
  SMS-agent / check-in orientation.
- **Per-session configuration chosen:** **2 courts, rotate** — 8 kids play
  (`rp_04` per court), 4 rotate through a drill station + reffing. This is the
  blueprint's kid-coach pillar doubling as a cost saver.
- **Per session (12 kids, 2-hr block):** court 2 × 2 × $17 = **$68** + coach
  **$100** + balls **$15** = **$183**.
- **Per cohort-season (8 sessions):** sessions $1,464 + admin $350 + capital
  (nets, amortized) $45 + fixed allocation (insurance / certs / marketing) $267 =
  **~$2,126 fully loaded**.
- **Breakeven ≈ $177/kid.** Pilot pricing at +30% → round to **~$240/season
  (~$30/session)**; after ~3% Stripe + a small weather buffer this nets ~30%.
- **Owner take at $240 × 12 kids** ($2,880, −~$86 Stripe): your $800 coach wage +
  $350 admin + ~$668 profit above all costs ≈ **~$1,818 per 8-week cohort**.
- **Flag:** the $267 fixed line and the capital line are still **estimates pending
  real quotes** — insurance is the big swing (see the gap register).

## 3. Minimum viable roster (per division)

Costs are almost entirely **fixed per season**, so "minimum kids" is really "how
much of the fixed cost the roster covers." At small headcount, drop to a
**1-court config** (4 play / 1–2 rotate), which halves the biggest line. Hard
out-of-pocket for a 1-court season ≈ **$650** (court $272 + balls ~$90 + 1 net
$25 + fixed $267). At the $240/kid price:

| Kids | Revenue | After hard costs | Your effective wage | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | $720 | $70 | ~$2/hr | Cash floor — stop losing money, work free |
| 4 | $960 | $310 | ~$11/hr | Format floor (full partner round-robin), near-volunteer pay |
| 5 | $1,200 | $550 | ~$20/hr | Costs covered + modest pay |
| 6 | $1,440 | $790 | ~$28/hr | Costs covered + decent pay |
| 8 | $1,920 | $1,270 | ~$45/hr | Full freight (your ~$50/hr target) |

**Recommendation: 6 to go, 5 conditional, 4 absolute floor** — anchored on an
absence buffer (6 enrolled − 1 typical absence = 5 present, still above the 4-kid
format floor).

**Merge rule:** any division that closes registration under 5 **merges with the
adjacent age band** (the all-levels-Tuesday model already proves
mixed-band-per-court works) rather than run thin or cancel.

**Config scales with headcount:** 4–6 kids → 1 court; 7–8 → 1–2 courts; 9–12 → 2
courts.

## 4. Prioritized gap register (P0 → P4)

### 🔴 P0 — Existential. Resolve before taking a dollar or putting a kid on a public court.

1. **Right-to-operate.** Does a CUPF / MCPS court-rental permit cover running a
   *paid commercial program*, or just renting a court? For-profit reclassification
   means they already know you're commercial — but renting a court ≠ a
   vendor/concession right to *operate a business* on school grounds.
   → **Get explicit written confirmation from CUPF** (drafted email in Appendix A).
2. **Insurance.** General liability **+ abuse/molestation (SAM)** coverage. CUPF
   almost certainly requires a certificate of insurance naming the County as
   additional insured; SAM is also your personal-risk shield. Not yet
   quoted/bound. → **Get quotes** (Appendix B research).
3. **Legal entity + liability shield.** Is NGA an LLC, or is Sam personally
   exposed? An entity must exist before insuring or contracting with parents.
4. **Adult vetting.** Background checks + SafeSport-equivalent for Sam, the parent
   volunteers, and the 16U mentors. → Pick a vendor + standard; budget ~$25–50/adult;
   keep records.
5. **Enrollment paperwork.** Liability waiver + medical/emergency consent + photo
   consent per child. Taking $240 for an 8-week commitment with no signed release
   is unacceptable. → Draft (attorney-glanced) forms; collect at enrollment.

### 🟠 P1 — Launch-blocking ops & money.

6. **Indoor/winter venue — UNSOLVED, and it threatens the annual model.** CUPF has
   no indoor pickleball; MCPS closes outdoor courts ~Aug 15. If only spring–summer
   is runnable, the "~12 cohort-seasons/yr" annual picture collapses to a seasonal
   product. → Chase the Montgomery Parks indoor lead; re-base the annual model on a
   realistic season count.
7. **Recurring lined-court booking** — same school, same slot, 8 weeks. Only
   specific MCPS schools have painted pickleball lines; CUPF opening-day
   reservations are competitive with hard cut-offs. → Confirm a recurring 8-week
   series can be reserved at a lined court, and the opening-day timing.
8. **Weather buffer + refund policy as a real consumer contract.** MoCo summers
   lose nights to rain / heat advisories; the CUPF refund cliff (lose everything
   under 10 business days) is brutal. → Schedule 8 sessions across 9–10 weeks
   (built-in make-up) + publish clear season T&Cs (drop, you-cancel, weather,
   undersubscribed).
9. **Real fixed-cost quotes.** The $240 price rests on a placeholder $267 fixed
   line; insurance is the big swing. → Falls out of #2 + #4, then re-confirm price.
10. **Stripe season product + payment mechanics.** Only the $20 drop-in product
    exists. Need a season price, deposit vs. full, a payment-plan option, and the
    refund policy wired in.
11. **First-aid/CPR/AED + per-venue Emergency Action Plan + two-deep leadership
    rule.** On a public court Sam is the sole adult (single-adult-with-kids is
    itself a safeguarding gap; volunteers/mentors mitigate). → Get certified; write
    a per-venue EAP; make "never one adult alone" an operating rule.

### 🟡 P2 — Demand validation (cheapest, highest-info — do FIRST, in parallel).

12. **Can you actually fill a division?** The model assumes 6–12 kids/division; the
    pipeline is unquantified. → Pull the real lead / newsletter pipeline; gauge one
    band's interest *before* P0/P1 spend.
13. **Run the Phase 0 paper pilot on the all-levels Tuesday** — needs none of
    P0/P1 (existing drop-in cover). Validates the "parents pay for the growth
    curve" hypothesis at near-zero risk.
14. **Pick the pilot band** (likely 10U — the golden-skill age, easy to fill).

### 🟢 P3 — Curriculum & build (the polished/scaled product, not the paper pilot).

- Reconcile checklist items to the live shared-vocabulary CSV + coach-calibrate
  the anchors (is 10U too early for the kid-coach ref role? are the drop hit-rates
  real?).
- Build the `community-os` league app: identity join, `player_session_stat`
  growth record, badges.
- Cross-repo access — `community-os` / `nga-coaching-system` aren't in scope,
  blocking vocab reconciliation + the build.

### 🔵 P4 — Go-to-market polish.

- `/league` page (teased price, passes `verify-funnel.mjs`) + positioning vs. the
  existing drop-in / Yellow Ball / Crew funnel.
- Final badge names + passport print design.

## 5. The wedge (sequencing)

Run the **Phase 0 paper pilot under existing drop-in cover** (no new legal
surface) to validate demand *while* resolving P0 in parallel; only flip to a paid,
branded **"league season"** once P0 is cleared. The instant you take season money
and call it a "league," the whole P0 tier triggers — don't cross that line before
insurance + permit + waivers are in hand.

**Single most urgent:** nail down **#1 (right-to-operate)** and **#2 (insurance
quote)** — if either fails, the economics change before anything else matters.

## Appendix B — Maryland youth-sports insurance options (researched)

**Goal:** general liability **+ abuse/molestation (SAM)** coverage for a small
**for-profit** youth pickleball program in Maryland, with the ability to issue a
**certificate of insurance naming an additional insured** (the County / school
facility CUPF will require).

**The gating piece is SAM, not GL.** Standard general-liability policies
frequently **exclude or heavily limit** sexual-abuse/molestation claims, and SAM
is almost always a **separate add-on with its own supplemental application and
underwriter approval** — for a youth-contact program it is the coverage that
actually matters and the one most likely to slow you down. Budget for it
explicitly and apply early.
([ESP Specialty](https://www.espspecialty.com/blog/athletics/sexual-abuse-and-molestation-insurance-coverage-explained/),
[Sadler — SAM for sports orgs](https://www.sadlersports.com/blog/sexual-abuse-and-molestation-insurance-for-sports-organizations/))

### Avenue 1 — Sport-specific amateur-program broker (recommended: Sadler Sports & Recreation)

The most concrete fit for a small team/league/program operator, and the avenue
with **firm published rates**.

- **What it covers:** Amateur sports team/league GL. Sadler's **Class A** tier is
  **$3.75 per participant with a $300 minimum premium** for **$1,000,000 CGL**.
  *(firm, published)*
- **Abuse/molestation:** **Not auto-included** — a separate supplemental
  application goes to underwriters. Published options are **Option 1: $100,000
  defense-cost reimbursement** or **Option 2: $250,000 occurrence / $1,000,000
  aggregate**. SAM-specific premium is **quote-required**. *(coverage limits firm;
  premium quote-required)*
- **Additional insured / COI:** Yes — certificates can be self-issued 24/7 and add
  a **Property Owner/Lessor, Sponsor, Sports Governing Body, Certificate Holder,
  etc.** as additional insured (covering liability arising out of the insured's
  operations) — exactly the structure CUPF/County will ask for. *(firm)*
- **Caveat:** the team/league policy does **not** cover facility
  ownership/operation or non-rostered camps — fine for our rostered-cohort model,
  but worth confirming our 8-week paid program reads as a covered "program" and not
  a "camp" needing the separate camp/clinic form.
- **How to quote:** apply online via Sadler's amateur-sports program (instant
  proof-of-coverage docs) or call **800-622-7370** for the SAM supplemental.
  ([amateur program](https://www.sadlersports.com/amateur/),
  [pickleball page](https://www.sadlersports.com/pickleball-insurance/),
  [SAM options](https://www.sadlersports.com/samcoveragedirect/))

### Avenue 2 — Pay-as-you-go instructor GL (Thimble)

Best if we want **month-by-month** coverage that matches a seasonal product rather
than an annual bind.

- **What it covers:** Business GL for pickleball instructors — bodily injury /
  property damage during lessons. Coverage by the **job, month, year, or event**;
  GL limits in the market run **$1M–$5M**. *(coverage firm; exact premium
  quote-required — Thimble prices per quote)*
- **Additional insured / COI:** **Add additional insureds and issue unlimited
  certificates of insurance for free** — directly useful for the CUPF/County
  requirement. *(firm)*
- **Abuse/molestation:** Verify SAM is available/included before relying on it for
  a youth-contact program — a general instructor GL policy may not carry SAM, which
  is the gating coverage here. *(verify at quote)*
- **How to quote:** instant online quote at
  [Thimble — pickleball instructor](https://www.thimble.com/industry/fitness-business-insurance/pickleball-instructor).

### Avenue 3 — Coaching-certification affiliate insurance (PPR / PCI)

Bundles **basic liability with a coaching credential** — worth weighing since the
credential has independent value, but **confirm it's enough on its own** for a
for-profit youth program.

- **PPR (Professional Pickleball Registry):** membership **$281** (through Aug 31,
  2027) includes **$25,000 occupational accident** + **$8,000,000 personal
  liability** upon certification & safe-play approval; additional
  policies/facility certificates can be added at checkout. *(membership cost +
  included limits firm; add-on costs quote-required)*
  ([PPR pricing](https://pprpickleball.org/pricing/))
- **PCI (Pickleball Coaching International):** intermediate+ certification members
  can **request US-based liability insurance certificates at no extra charge**.
  *(certificate availability firm; limits/exclusions quote-required)*
  ([PCI insurance](https://www.pickleballcoachinginternational.com/pages/insurance))
- **Caveat:** these are coach-centric credential perks — likely **need topping up
  with SAM + a higher-limit GL + a named-additional-insured COI** to satisfy CUPF.
  Treat as a complement, not the whole stack.

### Bottom line for the quote step (gap-register #2 / #9)

- **Firm numbers in hand:** Sadler Class A GL at **$3.75/participant, $300 min**
  for $1M CGL; Sadler SAM option limits ($100k defense-cost, or $250k/$1M); PPR
  membership **$281** with included limits.
- **Quote-required:** the **SAM premium** (the real swing on our $267 fixed line),
  Thimble's month-by-month premium, and any PPR/PCI add-on COI costs.
- **Recommended path:** get a Sadler amateur-program quote **with the SAM
  supplemental** as the primary, price a Thimble month-by-month policy as the
  seasonal-flex alternative, and only lean on a PPR/PCI credential bundle if it can
  produce a County-additional-insured COI **with** abuse/molestation coverage.
  Re-confirm the ~$240 price against whichever SAM-inclusive quote comes back.

*No premium figures above are invented — where a credible source published a firm
rate it's marked firm; everything else is flagged quote-required.*
