## 1. Hero Audience

### Primary: Parents (35–55)

The decision-maker. Pays, drives, asks the hard questions.

- Seeks safe, structured activity with **visible progress** for their child.
- Values family connection and shared development — pickleball is a vehicle, not the end.
- Values fitness, athletics, and health modeled at home.
- Pain: youth sports that don't take pickleball seriously, rec leagues built for adults, and clinics that lump a 7-year-old and a 14-year-old in the same drill line.

### Secondary: Athletes (5–16)

The end user. Comes back because it's fun, learns because it's structured.

- Wants fun + mastery + peer connection (in that order).
- Coachable, but needs a reason to listen.
- Responds to the **ball-color pathway** — visible level, visible progress.

### Tertiary: Local partners (schools, clubs, facilities)

- Need turnkey programming (after-school, summer camp, lesson plans).
- Equipment, coaches, curriculum, facility — we bring the full stack.

*A family-driven community for learning that develops pickleball skills and strategies through a year-round program offering weekly drills, games, and tournaments with other youth players of similar skill level for competitive and challenging growth on and off the court.*

## 3. Tagline

# Better than yesterday—together.

- Locks two ideas at once: **growth** (better than yesterday) and **family** (together).
- Works on every surface: website hero, email signoff, t-shirts, newsletter, end-of-session debrief cue.
- Pairs with "Next Gen Pickleball Academy" as a period, not as a mid-sentence stuff.
- Dovetails with EASE (see §4) — every session is a step toward "better than yesterday."

**Use as:**
- Website hero headline
- Email signature tag
- Closing line of end-of-season reports
- Rallying cry in coach huddles

**Don't use as:**
- A mid-sentence filler ("Our better-than-yesterday-together approach means…")
- An ironic or punchline setup

## 4. Core Values — EASE

Every email, landing page, newsletter section, and coach note should ladder to one of these. (EASE is NGA's equivalent of LD's 3 pillars — same role, different framework.)

1. **Ethics** — We model integrity, respect, and fair play.
2. **Attitude** — Growth mindset, positivity, resilience.
3. **Skills** — Purposeful progress along a clear pathway (Red → Orange → Green → Yellow).
4. **Excellence** — Preparedness, consistency, pride in details.

Hashtag: **#PlayWithEASE** (end of social captions to reinforce).

## 5. Voice — The Coach (NGA register)

**Voice:** Up-tempo, encouraging, specific. Coach-like, never jargon-heavy.

Sounds like Sam or Amine on a sideline at Rockville or North Bethesda — specific cue, positive tone, next step.

### Tone splits by audience

| Audience | Setting | What it sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| **With kids** | Playful, clear, actionable | "Two quick dinks, then reset. Go." |
| **With parents** | Reassuring, transparent, progress-oriented | "Morgan moved from Orange to Green after 4 weeks — here's what he's working on next." |
| **With partners** | Turnkey, confident, logistics-first | "8-week program, 12 kids, two coaches, curriculum delivered. You plug us in, we run it." |

### Tone dials (target: 4-4-2-4-3)

| Dial | Setting | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Formal ←→ **Casual (4)** | Contractions yes. "Nice one!" not "Well executed." |
| Serious ←→ **Playful (4)** | Kids need play; parents tolerate playfulness when it's paired with progress receipts. |
| Restrained ←→ **Expressive (2)** | One emoji max per message. No ALL CAPS. Energy comes from words. |
| Humble ←→ **Confident (4)** | "This is the level for her" not "might be." Parents pay for clarity. |
| Inclusive ←→ **Aspirational (3)** | "Real progress" inclusive; ball-color pathway signals quality without gatekeeping. |

### Words we use a lot
*pathway, progress, reps, cues, EASE, triangle, community, better than yesterday, ready, next step, eval, Red/Orange/Green/Yellow*

### Words we avoid
- Hype without proof ("AMAZING!!", "EPIC!!")
- Sarcasm
- Insider pickleball slang parents won't know (no "dink," "reset," "ATP," "Erne," or "stack" without a one-line gloss)
- Corporate filler ("We're pleased to inform…")

### Voice in practice

**Do (to a parent):**
> *"Morgan did great with the two juniors last Saturday — fits the Green group. Same time next week? We'll work on split-step and cross-court dinks."*

**Do (to a kid in a reel caption):**
> *"Third shot drop. 5 reps. See if you can land 3 in the kitchen. Go."*

**Don't (too hyped):**
> *"🔥 EPIC day on court today!! Your kid CRUSHED it!! 🎾💪 Amazing work!!"*

**Don't (too formal):**
> *"We are pleased to report that your child has demonstrated satisfactory progression in the current developmental tier."*

## 6. Voice Rules

Hard rules. Any violation = ❌ in `/brand-review-nga`.

### Writing rules
- **"You" (the parent) is the anchor.** "Your child," "your son/daughter," "your family."
- **Specific over vague.** Names, ages, session dates, color band, skill milestone — not "great progress awaits."
- **Pickleball jargon is gated.** One-line gloss on first mention, or drop it. Parents aren't pickleball people yet.
- **Kid copy uses action verbs and short sentences.** "Hit 5 dinks. Now 5 more."
- **Times in Eastern** (ET / EST), never UTC.

### NGA-specific divergences from LD
- **"Free evaluation" IS allowed as a CTA.** The first eval is genuinely free for all NGA leads (`feedback_eval_pricing`) and it's NGA's primary conversion lever. This is a deliberate divergence from LD §3.5 ("Never include 'free' in a CTA").
- **Email CCs:** Always CC `nextgenacademypb@gmail.com` on NGA outbound. Non-negotiable.
- **Signature:** Co-founders. Use "Coach Sam" and "Coach Amine." Do NOT use "Sam Morris—Head Coach" (stale from the Aug 2025 guide).
- **Age range:** 5–16. Lead forms, copy, and partner decks all align on this. (Prior drift had 4+/8–16 — canonical is 5–16 as of 2026-04-18.)

### CTA conventions
- Lead with a verb where possible. "Book a free evaluation" > "Free evaluation available."
- Parent-facing CTAs do one of: book eval, register for season, reply with questions.
- Kid-facing CTAs are drill-centered: "Try this 5x," "Post your best streak," "Tag a friend who should try this."
- Banned phrases: "Learn More," "Click Here," "Submit," "Get Started" (SaaS smell).

### Email subject line (parents)
- ≤ 50 characters preferred, ≤ 60 absolute max.
- Lead with child's age or name-placeholder, or a concrete time: "Your 7-year-old's first eval — pick a time."
- Never start with "Next Gen Pickleball Academy —" (burns 28 chars on brand that's already in the From name).

### Evaluation outcome framing
Every evaluation report (coach → parent) MUST include:
- **One specific win** ("Morgan's split-step is already clean.")
- **One focus area** ("Cross-court dinks still drift long — that's our next 4 weeks.")
- **Placement** (color band + why)
- **Next step** (specific session date/time to register for)

## 7. The Ball-Color Pathway

NGA's visible, shared language for skill. Kids see the color, parents see the stage, coaches see the curriculum. The pathway itself is brand — it doubles as the color palette (see Part 1).

| Color | Stage | What it means | Typical age | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 **Red** | Level 1 — Discovery | First paddle in hand. Oversized foam balls, low nets, micro-court. Basic rallying, grip, ready position. | 5–7 | Can sustain a 5-hit rally with a coach feed; knows serve → return → cross-court. |
| 🟠 **Orange** | Level 2 — Foundation | Larger balls, bigger court, kid-height nets. Serve + return consistency, forehand & backhand groundstrokes, intro to dinks. | 7–10 | Can play a game to 7 against a peer without a coach feeding. |
| 🟢 **Green** | Level 3 — Development | Full court, transition balls. Volleys, third shots, positioning, basic strategy. Starting to compete. | 9–13 | Consistent third-shot drop in drills; understands stacking; can keep score. |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | Level 4 — Competition | Full rules, full speed. Tournament prep, doubles strategy, ATP/Erne exposure, mental game. | 12–16 | DUPR-eligible (14+), can hold their own at junior tournaments. |

**When to evaluate up:**
- Monthly mini-eval inside the pathway (coach tests 2–3 skills + 1 EASE behavior).
- End-of-season formal review.
- Parent-requested re-eval (billable as a private lesson — see `feedback_eval_pricing`).

**The pathway is the progression; never skip without an eval.**

## 8. Parent–Coach–Kid Triangle (NGA's Operating System)

Unique to NGA. No LD analogue. The triangle is the thing that differentiates NGA from "kids clinic at the club down the road."

### Roles

**Parents — "Learn • Play • Support"**
- Learn the game (Parent 101/201 clinics)
- Play yourself (1+ adult session/month)
- Play with your kid (20–30 min family play once/week; drills provided)
- Build community (Parent Ladder or "Parents & Paddles" social once/season)
- Goal-setting partner (co-create season + weekly micro-goals)
- Execute (1 at-home drill + 1 play block weekly)
- Evaluate together (post-session reflection; monthly check-ins)
- Stay informed (weekly parent newsletter)

**Coaches — "Teach • Guide • Connect"**
- Teach parents (monthly Parent 101/201; rule/etiquette videos)
- Guide goals (approve season + weekly micro-goals)
- Provide plans (10-min home drills + 30-min family play plans weekly)
- Connect community (Parent Ladder, Family Play Night, parent buddies)
- Evaluate (monthly skill checks + parent-friendly progress notes)
- Communicate (30 min/week virtual office hours + newsletter FAQs)

**Kids — "Own • Practice • Reflect"**
- Own the goal (knows season goal and this week's micro-goal)
- Practice with family (10-min drill + 1 family play block weekly)
- Reflect (30-sec post-session "what got better / what's next")

### 90-day cadence (per season)

- **Week 0:** Parent Orientation (45 min) + Parent 101 (45 min); set family season goals.
- **Weekly:** Coach posts Skill Focus + EASE Focus + home drill + family play plan. Kids do drill, parents log ✓.
- **Biweekly:** 5-min coach note in team chat: win / focus / next step.
- **Monthly:** Parent 201 (45 min) + athlete mini-eval + check-in (5–8 min).
- **Season end:** Progress report + next-level recommendation + celebration play day.

### Simple scorecard (0–3)
Technique • Consistency • Decision-making • EASE habit
0 = not yet | 1 = emerging | 2 = consistent in drills | 3 = consistent in play

## 9. Founders' Note (Our Why)

Next Gen was started by two dads — **Sam and Amine** — building a roadmap for our own kids: structured lessons, a positive culture, and a clear path from curiosity to competition. We created the place we wanted for our families — a safe, inclusive community where parents, coaches, and kids work as a team, and where "Next Gen" means both the **next generation** of players and a commitment to being **better than yesterday**.

### Coach Sam

Sam and his son Kobe happily spend hours each week at home throwing, catching, chasing, tagging, hitting, and playing. This disorganized but purposeful play has helped Kobe develop key fundamental physical and mental skills — so much so that he started pickleball at age 5, playing games. Sam is a former physical education teacher, a father to two boys (Kobe and Owen), and Director of Pickleball Programming at Dill Dinkers — the first dedicated indoor pickleball facility in Montgomery County (Rockville + North Bethesda locations). Sam has been playing pickleball since 2019 and coaching since 2021.

### Coach Amine

**Abdesslam Lahlou-Amine — Co-Founder, Co-Director.**

Coach Amine brings over 30 years of experience to the court, with a lifelong passion for racquet sports. Originally from Casablanca, Morocco, he began playing tennis at 15 and went on to compete at a high level, winning multiple K-Swiss Ultimate Tennis league championships. Before tennis, he was already excelling in racquet sports — as a junior, he won many table tennis tournaments in Casablanca.

After moving to the U.S., Coach Amine attended the University of South Florida, where he earned his degree in Industrial Engineering. He built a successful career as an engineer with UPS and as a business owner before dedicating himself full-time to coaching and player development.

Coach Amine has trained athletes of all ages — from young juniors learning the fundamentals to adults sharpening their competitive edge. He is a USPTA Certified Tennis Professional and a PTR Certified Coach, and is currently working toward the prestigious Elite USPTA Certification. His coaching emphasizes strong foundations in footwork, technique, strategy, and mental toughness.

Coach Amine has also applied his coaching expertise at home. He began training his son Ryan at the age of four, guiding him step by step through the stages of junior tennis. Through this coaching, Ryan developed into a high-level competitor, winning multiple tournaments and achieving a strong UTR — a testament to Coach Amine's player development approach.

When Coach Amine was introduced to pickleball, he quickly developed a love for its speed, strategy, and fun. He introduced his son Ryan to the sport, and together they embraced it as a new competitive passion. Inspired by the sport's rapid growth and its appeal to players of all ages, Coach Amine now brings the same proven strategies he uses in tennis — building strong fundamentals, discipline, and confidence — to develop the next generation of pickleball players.

Recognizing the sport's explosive growth and the need for structured junior programming, Coach Amine partnered with Coach Sam Morris to launch Next Gen Pickleball Academy. Together they've created a unique progression system — similar to the red, orange, green, and yellow ball pathway in tennis — to help juniors develop in a structured manner while keeping the game fun and engaging. Their mission is simple: to inspire confidence, discipline, and a lifelong love for racquet sports in every player who steps on the court.
